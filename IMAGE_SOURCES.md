## Nigerian-Specific Sources

### 5. Local Nigerian Photographers on Instagram
Search hashtags:
- #NigerianFood
- #NaijaFood
- #AfricanVegetables
- #9jaFood

### 6. Take Your Own Photos
**Best option for authenticity!**
- Visit a local market (Mile 12, Bodija, Ariaria, etc.)
- Take high-quality photos of:
  - Yam tubers
  - Fresh vegetables (ugwu, waterleaf, scent leaf)
  - Beans and grains
  - Peppers and tomatoes
- Use natural lighting
- Clean background or blurred market scene

## Image Requirements

### Technical Specs:
- **Format**: JPG, PNG, or WebP
- **Minimum size**: 800x800 pixels
- **Recommended**: 1200x1200 pixels or higher
- **Aspect ratio**: Square (1:1) or landscape (4:3)
- **File size**: Under 2MB for faster loading

### Quality Tips:
- ✅ High resolution
- ✅ Good lighting
- ✅ Clear focus on the product
- ✅ Natural colors
- ✅ Clean or minimal background
- ❌ Avoid watermarks
- ❌ Avoid cluttered backgrounds
- ❌ Avoid blurry images

## How to Add Images

### Method 1: Via Sanity Studio (Easy)
1. Go to http://localhost:3000/studio
2. Click on "Product" > Select a product
3. Scroll to "Images" field
4. Click "Upload" or drag and drop
5. Click "Publish"

### Method 2: Bulk Upload Script (Faster)
1. Create folder: `public/product-images/`
2. Download/add your images to this folder
3. Name images to match products:
   ```
   white-yam.jpg
   cassava.jpg
   sweet-potato.jpg
   ugwu.jpg
   waterleaf.jpg
   plantain.jpg
   etc.
   ```
4. Run the upload script:
   ```bash
   npm run upload:images
   ```

## Recommended Images for Each Product

### Tubers & Roots:
1. **White Yam** - Whole tuber with rough brown skin
2. **Yellow Yam** - Cut to show yellow flesh inside
3. **Cassava** - Fresh roots, preferably peeled to show white interior
4. **Sweet Potato** - Orange-fleshed variety
5. **Cocoyam** - Brown corms with fibrous roots

### Vegetables:
6. **Ugwu** - Fresh green pumpkin leaves, bunched
7. **Waterleaf** - Fresh green leafy bunches
8. **Scent Leaf** - Green aromatic leaves
9. **Bitter Leaf** - Fresh green leaves
10. **Garden Egg** - White or green garden eggs

### Grains:
11. **Ofada Rice** - Brown/unpolished rice in bag or bowl
12. **Maize** - Yellow corn kernels or dried corn
13. **Guinea Corn** - Brown/red grain

### Legumes:
14. **Brown Beans** - Honey beans in bag or bowl
15. **White Beans** - White oloyin beans
16. **Groundnuts** - Shelled raw peanuts

### Spices:
17. **Cameroon Pepper** - Dried red hot peppers
18. **Locust Beans** - Dark fermented beans
19. **Ogbono** - Ground powder or whole seeds

### Fresh:
20. **Tomatoes** - Fresh red local tomatoes
21. **Scotch Bonnet Pepper** - Fresh hot peppers (atarodo)
22. **Plantain** - Unripe green plantain bunch

## Pro Tips

1. **Consistency**: Use similar photography style across all products
2. **Background**: White or wooden surface works best
3. **Lighting**: Natural daylight or soft white light
4. **Angle**: Slightly overhead or 45-degree angle
5. **Context**: Include market baskets or traditional containers for authenticity

## Quick Start (5 Minutes)

1. Visit [Pexels.com](https://www.pexels.com)
2. Search for each product name
3. Download in "Medium" or "Large" size
4. Save to `public/product-images/` folder
5. Run `npm run upload:images`
